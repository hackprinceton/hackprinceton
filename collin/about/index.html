ABOUT ME

I am a rising senior majoring in computer science at Princeton University. Relevant coursework includes networking, HCI, computational and algorithmic reasoning, functional and object-oriented programming, and system design.

This summer I'm an intern for Microsoft's Bing team. I previously interned at TripAdvisor as a member of the product personalization team.

Studying computer science was a gut decision for me. I started my career at Princeton as an aerospace engineer and changed majors after having my mind blown by an introductory course in Java. I've never looked back.

My current area of focus is on technologies which do a better job of collecting or presenting data in context. These technologies include "smart things" and wearables, note-taking and personal efficiency applications, and content recommendation systems. I want computers to see me as more than a bag of words or properties. Success will yield a digital world which is more intuitive and accessible from our own.

I'm also interested in digital rights and the structure of the Internet Society. In particular, I wish to study how the interaction of technology and policy affects the expression of individuals and groups on the Internet. I want to design "socially conscientious technologies" which improve the nature of communication among digital neighbors.

In my free time I like to juggle, play poker, and Make Things. I'm also an avid hackathon attendee. I do much of my web development with a CERN stack (Cassandra, Express, React, Node.js).
